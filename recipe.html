About this Recipe

Spaghetti is a staple of Italian cuisine: long, thin, solid, cylindrical pasta made primarily from durum wheat semolina and water. The name comes from the Italian word spago, meaning "string," from which the plural form, spaghetto, derives, meaning "little string".
